Case study Automation Budget: lean Fractional

One person. Two ventures. Hiring that actually worked.

Two AI ventures · Same founder · Shared team · Remote-first

Two ventures, one founder — a product team and an outsourcing stream, operating in parallel under shared leadership. One fractional HR person running hiring for both.

Both ventures were early, fast-moving, and hiring for roles in a niche talent market. The challenge wasn't just finding the right people — it was building a single process that could serve two business units, run lean, move fast, and still make good decisions without burning the founder's time or the budget.

What was built

Every role started with a structured founder intake — a conversation to pressure-test whether the hire was actually needed, align expectations before the search began, and build a clear picture of what good looked like for that specific role and environment.

A custom ATS was built on existing tools, keeping infrastructure costs near zero. The admin work was automated where it made sense — candidate self-booking, automatic profile creation, AI-transcribed screening calls with structured summaries ready before the second round. The parts that required human judgment — sourcing, vetting, culture assessment — stayed human.

Scorecards and interview plans were built per role. Every hire was assessed not just on skills but on the ability to work in a high-uncertainty, early-stage environment — because that's what both ventures actually needed.
